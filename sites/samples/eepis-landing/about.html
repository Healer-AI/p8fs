About EEPIS

Building the future of secure personal memory infrastructure

Our Mission

EEPIS exists to give you complete control over your digital memory. We built a system where your data belongs to you, protected by end-to-end encryption with keys that never leave your devices.

Traditional cloud storage requires trusting companies with your private information. EEPIS uses zero-knowledge architecture where we cannot access your content even if we wanted to. Your mobile device generates the cryptographic keys. Only you hold the private key.

The REM System

Resource-Entity-Moment architecture represents a new approach to personal memory management. The system extracts temporal narratives from your content, identifies semantic relationships between entities, and builds an intelligent knowledge graph.

Our System Agent processes documents, conversations, and media through dreaming workflows that consolidate memories while maintaining full encryption. Vector embeddings enable natural language queries across your entire vault.

Security Architecture

EEPIS implements defense-in-depth security:

  • Client-side encryption with mobile-generated keypairs
  • OAuth 2.1 device authorization flow eliminating password vulnerabilities
  • AES-256 encryption at rest in S3-compatible storage
  • TLS 1.3 with certificate pinning for transport security
  • Distributed storage on TiDB/TiKV with vector and graph capabilities

Technology Stack

EEPIS builds on proven technologies:

  • SeaweedFS for S3-compatible encrypted blob storage
  • TiDB/TiKV for distributed database with SQL, vector, and graph support
  • NATS JetStreams for scalable message processing
  • Kubernetes with KEDA for on-demand worker scaling
  • Native applications for iOS, macOS, and Android

Privacy Commitment

Your privacy is not negotiable. We designed EEPIS so that protecting your data is a technical guarantee, not a policy promise. Zero-knowledge architecture means we cannot decrypt your content. Mobile-first authentication means no password databases to breach. End-to-end encryption means your data stays yours.
